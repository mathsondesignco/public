### [2003-07-10](/news/2003/07/10/index.md)

#  NASA reports the discovery of PSR B1620-26 b (unofficially dubbed "Methuselah"), the oldest extrasolar planet yet discovered. The planet, which is estimated to be 12.7 billion years old, is orbiting the pulsar PSR B1620-26 in the core of the ancient globular star cluster M4, located 5,600 light-years away in the summer constellation Scorpius.
