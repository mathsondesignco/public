### [2004-04-4](/news/2004/04/4/index.md)

#  Thomas H. Kean, chairman of the commission investigating the September 11, 2001 attacks and former Republican governor of New Jersey says that the attacks could have been prevented had the United States government acted sooner to dismantle Al Qaeda and responded more quickly to other terrorist threats. 

The chairman of the commission investigating the attacks said that even as the White House sought to dispel the notion that the attacks were avoidable.
